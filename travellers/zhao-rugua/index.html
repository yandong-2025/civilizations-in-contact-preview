Summary

From 1224 to 1227, Zhao Rugua was Superintendent of Maritime Trade for Fujian circuit and for the city of Quanzhou, then a major Song dynasty port and commercial center.

His Zhu fan zhi is divided into sections on foreign polities and on trade goods. In the Rosewater material, he preserves earlier information about rosewater as a product associated with Ta-shi, or Arab lands.

For this prototype, Zhao Rugua functions as a textual witness who links Rosewater to Chinese maritime knowledge and to longer histories of tribute and foreign goods.

Rosewater from Ta-shi

“Zhao Rugua says rosewater was made from the dew of flowers in the country of the Ta-shi, identified in the legacy material as Arab lands.”

Friedrich Hirth and W. W. Rockhill, trans. and annot., Chau Ju-kua: His Work on the Chinese and Arab Trade in the Twelfth and Thirteenth Centuries, entitled Chu-fan-chi, Part II, chapter 10, pp. 203-204.

The legacy source also discusses rosewater as chiangwei-shui and notes comparisons with attar of roses.
